2. Home Sweet Home: The Gulf Coast üíô

üåé Global Range

  • Primary home: **Gulf of Mexico** (Mexico, Texas, Louisiana, Alabama, Florida).
  • They love **shallow, nearshore waters** and bays with soft, muddy or sandy bottoms.
  • Juveniles can travel far, sometimes carried by the Gulf Stream all the way to New England!

üìç Spotted in Alabama!

While not a primary nesting spot, the Alabama coast is important!

  • The coastal and bay waters (like Mobile Bay) are crucial **foraging areas** for younger turtles.
  • Occasional nesting has been documented on Alabama beaches (Mobile and Baldwin counties).

The "Arribada" Phenomenon

Kemp's Ridleys are famous for their **synchronized nesting events** called **arribadas** (Spanish for 'arrival'). Hundreds of females come ashore all at once! This tactic helps protect their eggs and hatchlings by overwhelming predators.

‚òÄÔ∏è Fun Fact: Daylight Nesters!

They are the **only** sea turtle species that consistently nests during **daylight hours** (April-July). They lay about $100$ eggs per nest, 2-3 times per season.

üê¢ How They Move

  • Ocean: They use their powerful front flippers for efficient, strong swimming.
  • Land (The Crawl): On the beach, they use an **alternating flipper movement** that creates a distinct, asymmetrical track in the sand.
  • Navigation: They are incredible navigators, believed to use the **Earth's magnetic field** to find their way back to their home beaches!

4. Tracking for Our AI Model üó∫Ô∏è

Data for Ocean Movement

For modeling **migration** and **foraging**, researchers use **Satellite Telemetry**. You can train your AI model on these data points:

  • **Latitude/Longitude:** GPS coordinates over time.
  • **Temporal Data:** Timestamp of location pings.
  • **Environmental Data:** Sea surface temperature, ocean currents (can be correlated with turtle movement).

Data for Beach Movement (Footprints)

The tracks left by nesting females are called **crawl tracks**. These are important for ground-level detection and confirmation of nesting.

**Footprint Characteristics:** The tracks show an alternating sweep of the front flippers (asymmetrical). A successful track leads to a "body pit" (where the nest is dug). For a model, focus on the **track width** and **angle of approach/return**.

5. How We Can Help! üíö

Biggest Threats

  • **Fishing Bycatch:** Accidentally caught in nets (especially shrimp trawls). This is the *leading* cause of adult/juvenile death.
  • **Coastal Development:** Artificial lights confuse hatchlings trying to find the ocean horizon.
  • **Marine Debris:** Eating plastic and garbage mistaken for food.
