Endangered Alabama Turtles: Loggerhead Sea Turtle

AI Class Research Project (High School Level)

1. Overview: The Loggerhead ($Caretta$ $caretta$)

The Loggerhead sea turtle is named for its massive head and powerful jaws, adapted for crushing the shells of prey. It is the most common sea turtle species to nest on Alabama's beaches and is listed as Threatened by the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service.

Key Statistics

  • Average Adult Weight: $113$ kg ($250$ lb)
  • Average Carapace Length: Up to $92$ cm ($36$ in)
  • Status: Threatened (Nationally); Highest Conservation Concern (Alabama)

2. Habitat & Alabama Range

Loggerheads are widely distributed across the Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans. They primarily inhabit continental shelf waters, including bays, lagoons, salt marshes, and estuaries where they can forage for food.

Where They Are Found in Alabama

In Alabama, Loggerheads are the **most abundant** sea turtle species found in coastal waters. Their nesting occurs along the Gulf Coast beaches located in **Baldwin and Mobile counties**, specifically from the Florida border west to Dauphin Island. The majority of nesting is concentrated between Fort Morgan and Gulf Shores.

Diet

As strong carnivores, their large heads and powerful jaws are perfect for eating hard-shelled prey like conch, whelks, crabs, clams, and shrimp. Juveniles often eat jellyfish and floating mollusks.

3. Nesting and Reproduction

Nesting Season in Alabama

The Alabama sea turtle nesting and hatching season is typically from **May 1st to October 31st.** Females come ashore primarily at night during this period.

The Nesting Process

  • A female may nest between $1$ and $7$ times within a single nesting season, with an average of $4$ to $5$ nests.
  • She returns to the beach at intervals of approximately $14$ days to lay a new clutch.
  • The incubation period is temperature-dependent, usually ranging from $55$ to $60$ days.
  • Hatchlings usually emerge at night and use moonlight/starlight to orient themselves toward the sea.

4. Locomotion & Tracking Model Resources

Movement on Land: Creating the Track

Only female sea turtles come ashore to nest. They pull their heavy bodies through the sand using their front flippers in an **alternating (or asynchronous) motion**.

Loggerhead Track Characteristics:

  • **Alternating Marks:** The flipper scoop marks on one side do **not** line up evenly with the marks on the opposite side.
  • **Comma Shape:** The flipper marks are typically deep and comma-shaped.
  • **Tail Drag:** A distinguishable, thin, sinuous line is often present down the center, left by the turtle's tail.

5. Conservation & Media Resources

Key Conservation Threats

The primary threats to Loggerheads include:

  • **Bycatch:** Unintended capture and drowning in commercial fishing gear (trawls, longlines).
  • **Habitat Loss:** Coastal development and beachfront lighting (which disorients hatchlings).
  • **Vessel Strikes:** Collisions with boats, especially in shallow coastal foraging areas.
