The Endangered Turtles of Alabama

A look at Alabama's vulnerable reptile residents and the places they call home.

Why Conservation Matters

Alabama is home to a diverse array of freshwater and marine turtle species, many of which face critical threats. From the coastal nesting grounds of the sea turtles to the unique river systems and longleaf pine forests of inland species, habitat loss, pollution, and climate change are pushing these magnificent reptiles toward extinction. This project highlights five of Alabama's most endangered and threatened turtles, offering a glimpse into their lives, their unique habitats, and the urgent need for their protection. Explore the pages below to learn more about these fascinating creatures.

Kemp's Ridley Sea Turtle

Lepidochelys kempii

Habitat:

Shallow, nearshore coastal waters, Gulf of Mexico, occasionally nesting on Gulf Coast beaches.

Loggerhead Sea Turtle

Caretta caretta

Habitat:

Subtropical and temperate ocean waters, utilizing sandy Atlantic and Gulf beaches for nesting.

Gopher Tortoise

Gopherus polyphemus

Habitat:

Terrestrial Longleaf Pine Ecosystems, dry, sandy ridges in the lower third of Alabama.

Flattened Musk Turtle

Sternotherus depressus

Habitat:

Upper Black Warrior River system. Free-flowing creeks/small rivers with submerged rock and crevices.

Alabama Red-bellied Turtle

Pseudemys rubriventris

Habitat:

Mobile-Tensaw Delta and Mobile Bay drainages, preferring shallow, vegetated backwaters (fresh/brackish).
